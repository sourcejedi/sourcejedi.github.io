Exhibit 3: There are various measurement delays for Covid statistics.  ZOE use a 14-day average.  They use swab samples taken no later than 2 days ago (previously 4 days), to allow time for lab testing.  Covid also has an incubation period, which may be as low as 3 days on average for the latest variant.  And not everyone would test on their first day of symptoms, especially if they had to wait for a PCR kit in the post.

ZOE have often talked about events as affecting their current estimates, without waiting for the full measurement delay.  For example, they discussed the effect of schools starting September 1, while pointing to swab data from the 14 days up to September 4.  For a disease with an incubation period averaging over 3 days.  A large proportion of schools were not even open to pupils on September 1 or 2, due to teacher training.

## Exhibit 1

ZOE showed a graph of "Cold-like illness symptoms" v.s. "Covid with respiratory symptoms".  The "Covid" line had reached about 75% of the "colds" line.  The problem is, the line for "colds" was explained as meaning "non-Covid colds".  The graph actually meant Covid was less than half of all respiratory infections.

A week earlier, on 2021-12-22, the ZOE video said "One in two colds are COVID".  The "Covid" line was 50% of the "colds" line.  So this video was also wrong.  It should say Covid was 1 out of every 3 new "colds".

This is embarrasing, however I did not notice the earlier mistake either.  I suspect everyone was trying to enjoy Christmas!  I still think there is a lesson here, though ZOE do not seem to agree.  In science, the method should always be available alongside the results.

For example, if the method was available in the press release, the BBC would have been more likely to ask ZOE about the mistake in the first statement.  Instead of reporting ZOE's statement on Radio 4 and online.

ZOE made a reasonable attempt to define the method for the graph.  The problem is this detailed definition is not accessible from the video or press release.  The definition is buried in the daily report "for app users only".  (Also the report is a PDF file.  The PDF file is best viewed on a PC or tablet, but the app does not run on PCs running Windows or MacOS.  Tablets are expensive, and most people use the app on a phone.)
