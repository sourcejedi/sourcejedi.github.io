I found a contradiction in the estimates provided through the ZOE app. I.e. through this link:
https://covid.joinzoe.com/your-contribution?utm_source=App

This web page has a graph at the top, that says:
"79707 (68650 - 91989) new cases in UK on 29 November".

However if you scroll to the bottom and click "View full report sent to the government", the first graph "Daily new cases in the UK by vaccination status" says:
"79707 (75043 - 84516) cases in the UK".

The mid-points are the same, but the confidence limits differ.

So, one of these graphs must have the wrong information.  Or, the graphs actually show different things, but the graphs are not being presented with enough information, even in the full report. So readers cannot tell what the thing being graphed actually is, in either case.

I hope this contradiction can be resolved. For the benefit of all users, including for the government.
